Saalumarada Thimmakka

Mother Of Trees

Saalumarada Thimmakka enjoying the bliss of nature beneath the trees she grew.

Timeline of the Legend

  • Born in 1912 in Hulikal village, Thimmakka's journey is an extraordinary tale of love and commitment to nature.
  • Thimmakka, along with her husband, began planting banyan trees along a 4-kilometer stretch of highway in their village. Lacking children of their own, the couple nurtured these trees as their own offspring.
  • Late 20th Century-2000s: Thimmakka's work gained attention, and she received various awards, including the National Citizens Award of India, for her exceptional environmental contribution
  • 2014: The Government of Karnataka honored Saalumarada Thimmakka by planting saplings along a 5 km stretch from her village to Kudoor in her name.
  • 2019: She was recognized by BBC as one of the 100 most influential women globally.
  • 2021: Saalumarada Thimmakka passed away, leaving behind a legacy of environmental stewardship and inspiring generations to come.
  • Thimmakka's story became an inspiration for environmentalists and ordinary citizens alike.
